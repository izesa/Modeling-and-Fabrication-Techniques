## 1.Small Introduction.
CNC and Laser Cutting is a Modeling and fabrication technique which uses the Subtractive Approach of Fabrication.
**CNC** in full words means Computer Numerical Control which refers to the automated control machining tools such as drills, routers, etc using a computer program.

**Laser Cutting** is a Usually CNC-controlled, non-contact, thermal-based manufacturing process that uses a high-powered, focuses laser beam to melt, burn or vaporize materials.

Laser cutting employs a high-powered,focused beam to precisely cut. so it requires a larger amount of heat to melt that type of material that is going to be subtracted form its box.

## 3. What determines clean cuts, accuracy, and tool life?
### 1. CNC machining
#### Clean cuts
1. Proper cutting speed (RPM) and feed rate
2. Sharp tools (dull tools tear material)
3. Correct chip load
4. Proper cooling/lubrication (especially for metals)
#### Accuracy
1. Machine rigidity (stiff frame reduces vibration)
2. Tool calibration and zeroing
3. Backlash compensation
4. Stable workholding (clamps, vacuum table)
#### Tool longevity
1. Avoid overheating (use coolant or air)
2. Use correct tool for material (e.g., carbide for metals)
3. Proper depth of cut and step-over
4. Minimize tool chatter (vibration)
### 2. Laser cutting
#### Clean cuts
1. Correct laser power + speed balance
2. Proper focus (focal point must match material thickness)
3. Assist gas (air, oxygen, nitrogen clears debris)
#### Accuracy
1. Beam alignment
2. Machine calibration
3. Material flatness (warped sheets reduce precision)
#### Tool longevity (laser system)
1. Clean optics (lenses/mirrors)
2. Cooling system maintenance
3. Avoid overdriving the laser tube

## 4. Common fabrication strategies
### 1. Enclosures (electronics boxes, cases)
#### CNC strategies:
1. Pocketing for internal space
2. Fillets on internal corners (due to round tool)
3. Threaded inserts or tapped holes
#### Laser strategies:
1. Tab-and-slot joints
2. Press-fit designs (account for kerf)
3. Living hinges (for flexible designs in wood/acrylic)
### 2. Furniture
#### CNC:
. Large sheet cutting (plywood, MDF)
. Dog-bone fillets for internal corners
. Interlocking joints
#### Laser:
. Scale models or lightweight furniture
. Decorative patterns (engraving + cutting)

### 5. Structural components
#### CNC:
. Strong, load-bearing parts (metal or thick wood)
. Multi-pass machining for deep cuts
. Reinforcement ribs and pockets
#### Laser:
. Thin sheet metal parts
. Fast prototyping of brackets
. Foldable designs (cut + bend)

#### Take Home
1. CNC = strength + 3D shaping
2. Laser = speed + precision in 2D
3. Best results come from:
4. Correct parameters (speed, power, feed)
5. Good design for manufacturing (DFM)
6. Proper maintenance of tools/machine
